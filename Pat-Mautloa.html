Kagiso Pat Mautloa

Kagiso Patrick "Pat" Mautloa is a South African multimedia visual artist born on September 24, 1952, in Ventersdorp, North West. He is based in Johannesburg. Mautloa's artistic journey began in his early years, moving to Soweto at the age of two. He studied visual arts at various centers, receiving a bursary after his involvement in the Soweto Uprisings. In 1978, he studied at ELC Rorke's Drift Art and Craft Centre under Dan Rakgoathe.

After his education, Mautloa worked as a graphic designer and a professor. In 1981, he briefly worked as a banker but chose to pursue his passion for art. In 1985, during the State of Emergency in South Africa, he founded the Thupelo Workshops in Cape Town.

Mautloa's studio is at The Bag Factory in Johannesburg. He works figuratively and abstractly, incorporating traditional and unconventional materials, including found objects. He has participated in various residencies and received awards, such as the Vita Art award in 1991. His work is featured in public collections, including the Iziko South African National Gallery and Johannesburg Art Gallery.
